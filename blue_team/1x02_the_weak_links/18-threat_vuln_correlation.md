## Which Single Vulnerability Would Cause the Most Damage?

**Finding 007 — LDAP signing not required on ad-dc-01.** Every other finding on this list compromises *one* asset: the PHI database, one workstation, one device class, one backup copy. Finding 007 compromises the mechanism that authenticates *every* asset at once. Domain admin rights, gained through a widely-automated, zero-custom-tooling technique, let an attacker push a ransomware payload to every domain-joined host simultaneously via Group Policy — meaning ehr-srv-01, billing-srv-01, file-srv-01, and ad-dc-02 could all go down in the same coordinated action, not sequentially. That combination — the most realistic threat actor for this sector (a ransomware affiliate, TA-1) pursuing the exact technique that's their standard mid-chain objective industry-wide, against the one asset whose compromise grants simultaneous control over everything else, on a network with zero segmentation to slow any of it down — makes this the single most damaging finding in the report. The PHI database (Finding 003) and the backup NAS (Finding 015) are each catastrophic *destinations*; Finding 007 is the master key that reaches all of them, and everything else, at the same time.
